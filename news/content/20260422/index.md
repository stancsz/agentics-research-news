### ECLASS-Augmented Semantic Product Search for Electronic Components
**Sources:** arXiv
**Links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19664v1
**TL;DR:** This paper introduces a method for semantic access to industrial product data, which is essential for factory automation and emerging LLM-based agent workflows. The approach integrates ECLASS to help identify suitable electronic components.
**Agentic Focus:** Empowers LLM-based agents with structured industrial data for autonomous identification of physical components.

### Chat2Workflow: A Benchmark for Generating Executable Visual Workflows with Natural Language
**Sources:** arXiv
**Links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19667v1
**TL;DR:** The paper proposes a new benchmark for evaluating models on their ability to generate executable visual workflows from natural language. This addresses the challenge of creating reliable and controllable workflows for real-world industrial deployments.
**Agentic Focus:** Advances agentic automation by benchmarking the generation of controllable, executable visual workflows from language.

### InHabit: Leveraging Image Foundation Models for Scalable 3D Human Placement
**Sources:** arXiv
**Links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19673v1
**TL;DR:** This research presents InHabit, a method utilizing image foundation models to generate large-scale data of human placements and interactions within 3D scenes. This overcomes the scarcity of real-world motion capture data for training embodied AI.
**Agentic Focus:** Improves embodied agents' spatial understanding and simulation of realistic human-scene interactions in 3D environments.

### A-MAR: Agent-based Multimodal Art Retrieval for Fine-Grained Artwork Understanding
**Sources:** arXiv
**Links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19689v1
**TL;DR:** The paper introduces A-MAR, an agent-based approach for fine-grained artwork understanding and retrieval. It tackles the multi-step reasoning required over visual content, cultural, historical, and stylistic contexts.
**Agentic Focus:** Utilizes an agentic framework to perform complex, multi-step multimodal reasoning and retrieval across cultural datasets.

### PlayCoder: Making LLM-Generated GUI Code Playable
**Sources:** arXiv
**Links:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19742v1
**TL;DR:** PlayCoder explores the ability of Large Language Models to generate functional and playable GUI applications, specifically games. It addresses the gap in existing benchmarks regarding interactive visual code generation.
**Agentic Focus:** Enhances the capability of coding agents to autonomously generate, test, and deploy interactive graphical user interfaces.
